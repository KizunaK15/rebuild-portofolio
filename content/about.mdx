My name is Prima Aji Setyawan. I study D4 Electronics Engineering Technology at Politeknik Negeri Semarang, where hardware constraints and real-world deployment conditions shape every decision I make.

The work that means the most to me sits at the intersection of embedded systems and human outcomes. When stroke patients struggled to regain hand mobility using existing off-the-shelf devices — bulky, expensive, and disconnected from their therapists — I built the Post Stroke Smart Hand Gloves. The glove runs an ESP32-S3 with an LVGL interface, syncs therapy progress to a web database in real time, and dispatches session reports automatically via the WhatsApp API. That project won a Silver Medal at IIIEX 2025.

Beyond rehabilitation, I designed a PID-controlled water level system on Arduino Uno using a VL6180X LIDAR sensor, suppressing overshoot below 2% and keeping steady-state error under 2.5 mm — a result that demanded careful tuning and an honest understanding of the plant dynamics before writing a single line of control code.

I rely on AI tooling to accelerate the lower-level coding work so I can spend more time on architecture, signal integrity, and the questions that actually determine whether a system holds up in the field.

If you are building something that needs reliable hardware, a tight control loop, or a sensor-to-cloud pipeline that works in production, I want to hear about it.
